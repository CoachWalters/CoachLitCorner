Research-Based Literacy Strategies

Explore methods proven to support early reading and writing development, including:

  • Phonemic Awareness: Activities that help children hear and manipulate sounds.
  • Interactive Read-Alouds: Engaging children with books and questioning techniques.
  • Print Awareness: Teaching children about letters, words, and how print works.
  • Vocabulary Building: Introducing new words in meaningful contexts.

Spotlight Skill of the Month

Reading Fluency is the ability to read text accurately, at an appropriate rate, and with expression. It is essential for comprehension and engagement.

Key strategies include:

  • Repeated Reading with Feedback
  • Echo Reading
  • Incorporating Technology Tools
  • Performance-Based Reading Strategies (e.g., Readersâ€™ Theater)
  • Short, Structured Fluency Drills

These strategies support improved accuracy, rate, and prosody, helping students become confident, motivated readers.

Parent Tips for Literacy at Home

  • Read aloud daily, making it fun and interactive.
  • Encourage storytelling and conversations about books.
  • Use everyday moments to introduce new words.
  • Create a cozy, inviting reading space.
  • Play rhyming and sound games to develop phonemic awareness.
